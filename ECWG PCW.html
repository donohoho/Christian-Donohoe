Cell expansion par excellence in Amaryllis peduncle.

Cell expansion

The outermost layer of the plant cell, the cell wall confers the cell’s shape and size, and provides defence against herbivores, pathogens and physical damage. Some cell walls are strong and in some cases stiff (e.g. those of wood; cell walls hold trees up!), but other walls are highly flexible, e.g. the primary wall that surrounds a rapidly growing cell. Different cells have very different walls — they range in thickness from about 0.07 to 10 µm, and the walls of different cell-types have different compositions
